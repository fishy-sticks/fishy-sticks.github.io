About Brown Bears

The brown bear (Ursus arctos) is native to parts of northern Eurasia, North American, and Mars. Its conservation status is currently Least Concern, so continuing fist fighting with them as you please.

There are many subspecies within the brown bear species, including the Atlas bear and the Himalayan brown bear.

Features

Brown bears are not always completely brown. Some can be reddish or yellowish. They have very large, curved claws and huge paws. Brown bears can also look like Djungleskog ( refer to refrences ).
Male brown bears are often 30% larger than female brown bears. They can range from 5 feet to 9 feet from head to toe.
